The Art of the Map

A journey from a blank canvas to a world of detail.

↓ Scroll to see the process
01

The Grid on Canvas

To ensure perfect proportions, I translated a small map onto this large canvas using a grid system. This foundation allows me to scale every coastline and border accurately across the massive surface.

02

First Strokes

The journey of a thousand miles begins with the first drop of pigment. In this beginning stage, I establish the primary color palette and lay down the base layers of the oceans and continents.

03

The Halfway Mark

With the base colors set, the world begins to take shape. This is the stage where depth is added through shading and topographical details, bridging the gap between a sketch and a painting.

04

Completing the Color

Finishing the coloring process involves refining the transitions between land and sea. Every island and peninsula is now vibrantly rendered, awaiting the final layer of information.

05

The Final Labels

The final stage is the most meticulous: labeling every country. This transformation turns a piece of art into a functional map, providing the final layer of human geography to the natural landscape.
